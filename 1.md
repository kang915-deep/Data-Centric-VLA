## 📑 1. Abstract & Motivation

While large-scale Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models (e.g., OpenVLA, Octo) demonstrate remarkable zero-shot capabilities, fine-tuning them for specific, high-precision downstream tasks (like dexterous manipulation) remains highly compute-intensive. 

Constrained by limited compute resources (e.g., single V100/RTX GPUs), traditional full-parameter fine-tuning is impractical. This project proposes a **Data-Centric VLA pipeline** that shifts the focus from "scaling compute" to "optimizing data quality and training efficiency". By integrating automated data pruning and Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT), this framework enables rapid, resource-efficient VLA adaptation and seamless Sim-to-Real deployment.

## 🛠️ 2. Core Pipeline

The pipeline consists of three main modules: **Quality-Aware Data Collection**, **Lightweight Adaptation**, and **Closed-Loop Deployment**.

### Phase 1: Smart Data Collection & Quality-Aware Pruning
Raw teleoperation or sim-generated data often contains idle frames, erratic movements, or sub-optimal trajectories. "Garbage in, garbage out" is heavily magnified in constrained training environments.
*   **Kinematic Variance Thresholding:** Automatically strip out "pause" frames and anomalous velocity spikes during human demonstration.
*   **Semantic Consistency Filtering:** Utilize pre-trained Vision-Language models (e.g., CLIP/SigLIP) to compute the alignment score between the visual observation and the language instruction, filtering out irrelevant frames.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Compress the dataset volume by 60%-70% while maintaining or even improving the behavioral cloning (BC) policy performance.

### Phase 2: Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) on V100
To adapt a 7B-parameter VLA model on limited VRAM (e.g., 16GB/32GB), we employ extreme lightweight engineering.
*   **Frozen Backbone:** Freeze the massive Vision Encoder and LLM trunk.
*   **QLoRA (4-bit Quantization + LoRA):** Inject Low-Rank Adapters into the attention layers of the LLM and the Vision Projector. This drastically reduces optimizer states and gradient memory footprints.
*   **Action Head Tuning:** Fine-tune a lightweight Diffusion Policy or deterministic MLP head for high-frequency continuous action output.

### Phase 3: Sim-to-Real Deployment & Verification
*   **Simulation (Isaac Sim):** Build a digital twin environment. Utilize domain randomization (lighting, textures) to pre-train the adapted VLA model.
*   **Real-world Transfer:** Deploy the quantized VLA model on local edge computing devices (or an RTX-powered workstation) to achieve >10Hz real-time closed-loop inference.
